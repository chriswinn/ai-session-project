What this file is and how to use it

Claude has no memory between conversations. This handoff file gives the next Claude instance everything it needs to pick up exactly where you left off — without you having to re-explain your project from scratch.

To use:

  1. Fill every ▶ placeholder with your project's content. Delete placeholders as you go.
  2. Delete this usage guide block once you've read it.
  3. Fill in the Operating Core (§6) with your project's specific rules, methodology, constraints, or decision framework. This is what makes the handoff powerful — Claude will apply your rules without you re-stating them.
  4. At the start of your next session, upload this file and tell Claude: "Please read this handoff and resume from where we left off."
  5. Save a new version at the end of each session. Suggested filename: project-name-handoff-YYYY-MM-DD.html

Works from anywhere: Upload from your computer, Google Drive, iCloud, Dropbox, or any cloud storage. No special tools required.

§6 — Project operating core

This section is the most important one to fill out well. It tells Claude how to think about your project — your methodology, your standards, your constraints, your vocabulary. A Claude instance reading this should be able to make decisions consistent with your approach without asking you to re-explain everything.
